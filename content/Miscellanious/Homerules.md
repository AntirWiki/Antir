This world was built for Pathfinder 2e, and these are the rules I typically add:

# I Know Somebody
Your characters have been living in and around this location for quite some time, so it stands to reason that they've made some social connections in the area. At any relevant time, you can claim that your character knows somebody. Describe the NPC that you know (not one the party has already met - make up someone new!), then make a **Diplomacy** check. The DC will be determined by the GM based on your described relationship with the NPC. 

>**Crit Success**: The person is Helpful to you 
>**Success**: The person is Friendly to you 
**Failure**: The person is Indifferent to you 
**Crit Fail**: The person is Unfriendly towards you 

If it is unlikely that your character knows such a person - for example, someone who works in a specific shop the party learned of only moments ago - the GM may first require a **Society** check to confirm that your character does actually know such a person. 

_Example: The party is investigating a murderer who targets manual laborers in the capital city. A player with the Sailor background claims that they know somebody who works at the docks. This is a very likely scenario - a sailor spends much time at the docks after all. As such, the GM does not call for a Society check. Given this character's cheerful nature, the GM sets the diplomacy DC at 15. The player rolls a 26 - a critical success! The player and the GM decide that this NPC is a close family member of the player character, and as such is Helpful towards them. The party then heads to the docks to meet with this NPC._

# Hero Points
Hero points are not refreshed at the beginning of each session. Instead, you reset to one hero point at narrative checkpoints, which may occur mid-session. These checkpoints will still occur about once per session to preserve balance. 
# Plot Points
D20 rerolls aren't the only way to bend fortune in your favor. Hero points can be spent to reroll any roll (within reason), including damage or other non-d20s. 

_In addition,_ you may spend hero points to cause a coincidence in your favor. The scope depends on how many you spend, for example: 
- When chasing a criminal, you might spend one hero point to conveniently run into a guard patrol.
- You've managed to accumulate three hero points, and spend them all for a powerful ally to appear and rescue you from a potential TPK All applications of this rule are subject to GM discretion
