Each nation of Antir has its own stereotypes of Adventurers. In the [[Holy Kingdom]], adventurers are heroes that slay monsters and defeat villains for the good of all. In [[Zauberei]], they're like a crisis team that deal with strange beasts and magic gone wrong. For the [[Strioc an Athraithe]], adventurers are treated like specialty mercenaries and guides. [[Blackwing]] treats adventurers as elite strike teams to deal with specific threats the military can't handle.

Despite these varied views of adventuring, an adventurer's work is more or less the same in any nation: dealing with threats that the local authority cannot or will not handle. Adventurers spend their days delving dungeons, hunting monsters, investigating mysteries, and doing any other odd task they can get paid for. 

# The Adventurer's Guild
>[!quote] Standard guild recruitment pitch
>Welcome to the adventurer's guild. We offer services to adventurers of all kinds, individual or in teams, from heroes to mercenaries, all across the continent. Wherever your journey takes you, we're there to support. Need a cheap meal and a bed in a strange city? We're in every town worth putting on a map. Need help negotiating your pay for a job? We have experts who can squeeze out every last copper. Looking for work? We keep postings of local jobs, all vetted and up to date. 
>But most importantly, we've got a place with people who understand the life. If you're looking for advice, teammates, or just a chat with somebody who gets it, you'll find it here.
>
>Membership is 1 gold per month, and we also take a small cut of negotiations and job postings from the guild. You'll also get a badge to prove that you're official.

The adventurer's guild is an organization that spans across Antir, offering community and support for adventurers of all types.

The symbol of the Guild is a wolf and a dragon, entwined around a sword. It represents legendary heroes [[Valerie Bouchard|Corsair Molossus Valerie]] and [[Darios O'Mael|Sir Darios Scalemaster]], who are seen as the first modern adventurers.

The guild's headquarters are located in [[Dragun Mac Tire]].

## Services
### The Grand Leger
Collectively, guilds serve as hubs of information and resources. Beyond being excellent places to swap stories, each guild houses a Grand Ledger — a magically connected board, linked by arcane leylines to every other guild across the continent. The Ledgers carry local and national news, monster sightings, calls for aid, and even weather predictions.
### Guild Supply
All guilds provide basic shelter and stocked gear (potions, armor, weapons, scrolls), typically offered at discounted rates – or free, depending on one’s rank. The quantity and quality of these supplies vary with the guild’s size and standing. Some of the larger guilds even offer in-house healing services. 

## The Heart of all Guilds
At the core of each guild are the guiding philosophies of The Dragon and The Wolf.

The Dragon Philosophy, in honor of Scalemaster Darios O’Mael, championed the defense of the innocent, healing, meditation, renewal, and the mastery of patience.

>[!quote] Teachings of Scalemaster Darios O’Mael
>The dragon does not burn the village – it circles above, watching for what may come.

The Wolf Philosophy, in honor of Corsair Molossus Valerie Bouchard, embraced decisive action – facing threats head-on, relentless training, and the pursuit of strength.

>[!quote] Corsair Valerie Bouchard, to her trainees
>Wolves do not wait for danger to knock – they hunt it in the dark.

All guilds followed the principles of both philosophies, though some guilds, and even individual adventurers, leaned more toward one than the other. Additionally, some guilds offer services beyond martial efforts, with duties and responsibilities shaped by the specific needs of their local communities.

### Guildhearts
In order to ensure that guilds across Antir remain aligned with the adventuring guild’s overarching mission – and to maintain strict political neutrality – they are regularly evaluated by a specially selected council of trusted and reputable adventurers known as the Guildhearts.

First established by the guild’s founding sisters, the Guildhearts are entrusted with overarching authority across all guilds; any new guild must agree to abide by their oversight. Guildhearts often serve as the heads of notable guilds throughout Antir and are responsible for supervising all branches within a certain radius of their post. Any misconduct — wrongful deaths, corruption, or entanglement with local authorities — ultimately falls upon the Guildheart’s shoulders (see The Fall of the White Elk Guild, c. 1198). All prospective guild branches must be approved and established under the guidance of a Guildheart, following a thorough assessment of the branch’s efficiency and its ability to uphold the guild’s ideals. Upon establishment, a guild is then granted access to the main resource pool – gold, materials, talent, and more.

Guild members who have served their communities and respective branches with distinction for a considerable time may be chosen to apprentice under a Guildheart. After further evaluation and training, if accepted unanimously by the circle, they are formally instated as a Guildheart themselves.

Guilds are also bound by mutual defense pacts: within a set radius, branches are expected to come to one another’s aid in times of need.

## History

### Foreword  
Historians regard Cianan of Antir, the Wizard King, as the ruler whose brief yet tumultuous reign (c. 953 – 960 BR) marked a turning point that beckoned magical dangers upon the world. Prior to his rule, catastrophic otherworldly events were all but unheard of – or, if they had occurred, none lived to tell the tale.

Alturon’s defenses had stretched thin after the creation of the Silent Woods. Most of the kingdom’s forces were stationed at its borders, desperately holding back the necrotic roots that threatened to breach the capital’s walls. Thousands had already perished to the creeping blight. Autumn had vanished, its absence unexplained. No one in the Holy Kingdom understood why – or how – or what could be done to stop it.

The ban on magic had made matters worse for the kingdom. Wisdoms, once respected, were now viewed with the same suspicion as wizards. Many, driven mad by fae whispers, withdrew from society, and cooperation became scarce. Some witches even embraced the strange occurrences plaguing the land. The Zauberans avoided crossing into Stríoc an Athraithe or the Holy Kingdoms, wary of both persecution and the unknown horrors beyond their borders.

To the north, the owlfolk – Moondancers, in their own tongue – pushed further into contested lands, locked in endless war with the Halit peoples. To the south, dragons and the growing conflict with the southern continent remained an ever-present threat. Trade routes grew increasingly treacherous. Villages swelled as refugees sought shelter, for beyond the safety of their walls lay destruction. The economy withered. Hope dwindled. 

Each realm turned inward, consumed by its own crises. Every new catastrophe demanded more resources than the land could provide. The people of Antir – farmers, traders, craftsmen – became forgotten. 

The combined creep of the Silent Woods and the thinning barrier between realms invited all manner of bizarre creatures and even more bizarre forms of devastation. One such region was home to a small village, where twin elven girls - Rosaline and Kastenia Valentine - lived unaware that the slow infestation of outside forces would soon shape their destiny. On one terrible night, just as summer was to come to a close, their village was caught in the crossfires of a battle between fae courts –  salvation came in the form of a group of heroes, led by the legendary Corsair Molossus Valerie Bouchard and Scalemaster Darios O’Mael, who fought desperately to drive them back. Though the invaders were repelled, much of the village lay in ruin. The twin girls, now orphaned, had lost everything.

The heroes took them in, not just as wards, but as family – guiding them through their grief, training them in the ways of battle and survival, renewing their strength to keep going. And as the twins grew, so too did the fire for adventure in their hearts.

With delicate control over the fae-touched magic flowing within them, the sisters swore to dedicate their lives to protecting Antir – just as their mentors had before them. From their vow, the first adventurer’s guild was born.

### Dragún Mac Tíre - The First Guild  

>[!quote] Rosaline Valentine, guild founder
For the good of all Antir, no guild member shall act as a common mercenary, for theirs is a duty bound to the realm.

With Valerie and Darios’ guidance and patronage, the first adventurer’s guild was established 1168 BR, bordering between Stríoc an Athraithe and the Silent Woods. Branches of the guild then arose along the borders of the Silent Woods – except on the side of Alturon, where the kingdom’s own forces held the line. Its mission was simple: to protect and serve the people. From there, the guild spread across the continent, bringing aid to the people far and wide.

The twins and their companions retraced the paths of their mentors, returning to villages that had once been saved by the legendary heroes. They recruited from among the survivors – those who had seen devastation firsthand. Some joined with a thirst for vengeance and peril, others with a desire to rebuild and protect. Yet for all their services, the guilds asked for nothing in return except one promise: to spread word of their mission and reignite hope in the hearts of the Antirians. 

Today, Dragún Mac Tíre remains the headquarters for all guild activity across the continent, anchored by a formidable city-state of the same name that continues to spread outward. It has drawn youthful, passionate, and powerful individuals from across the realm, earning a reputation as an energetic, bustling city where dreamers go to stake their claim in history. The founding twins continue to oversee much of the city and have become figures akin to its queens.

### Stríoc an Athraithe Branches
As one of the most dangerous yet central regions for continental travel and trade, the Athraithe region has become the prime hub for adventuring guilds, offering new beginnings to those shaped by its peculiarities. Witches who honor the old ways – working alongside spirits and upholding the Coven’s original purpose – are especially welcome and run many of these guilds. They strive to restore the ancient teachings of the Wisdoms while carefully navigating their relationships with the more fae-favorable witches of the ever-growing nation of Catsi Crossing. Dragún Mac Tíre, the first guild raised by the twin founders and the center of Guild administration, has built up a small city hub around it over the years.

The guilds in this region provide adventuring as a stable career, with no shortage of work to be done and plenty of wealthy patrons willing to pay for their services. There is roughly an equal balance of magic and non-magic casters. 

Additional duties include, but are not limited to: Escorting travelers/merchants, charting and updating maps as it shifts, defending against fae incursions (and activities of their witch sympathizers), engaging in diplomatic efforts with the Catsi Crossing nation, containing the spread of the Silent Woods, providing refuge and support to those warped by fae/occult/primal magic, aiding in the restoration and maintenance of the region’s natural health, including collaboration with local spirits and efforts to reclaim corrupted lands from fae control.

### Holy Kingdom Branches

>[!quote] 
>A blade drawn in rage is a curse. A blade drawn in service is a prayer.

Guilds remain sparse around the capital, as most of the region is under Alturon’s protection. Their presence receives mixed reception from the general public.

Whispers persist that Alturon’s leadership has quietly worked to dissolve adventurer’s guilds in this region, citing claims that they endanger commonfolk, shelter heretical mages, and siphon valuable talent away from the military and royal guard – all in exchange for uncertain loyalties and uncertain pay. House Bouchard has vehemently denied any involvement with the guilds and has maintained this stance over the decades. 

Despite the capital’s resistance, a handful of guilds continue to operate – generally under the front of charity or mercenary services, and are occasionally called on by villages when expertise beyond the villages’ guard is needed. 

These guilds tend to function on a volunteer or on-call basis, and are most active during the late summer months, in anticipation of the sudden onset of the sudden winter, when villages often require aid with the sudden transition.

Additional duties include, but are not limited to: Drakos ruin delving, serving as traveling healers and clerics to underserved villages, escorting travelers/merchants, offering education to remote settlements (often through church collaborations), maintaining or restoring holy sites, shrines, roads, cataloguing of magical anomalies (generally in collaboration with Zaubereian guilds).

### Zauberei Branches

>[!quote] 
>Let the arcane serve the many, not the few. Let knowledge be shared, not hoarded. Let power bow to purpose.

The Zauberei Branches were some of the last guilds to be established, to modern date. 

 As the adventuring guilds grew in both size and renown – along with an increased need for funding – it began to draw the attention of Foxfire Academy and the wizard city of Zauberei. The academy had long held interest in the Athraithe region since the Autumn King’s banishment, rich with volatile magical properties, ancient artifacts, and untapped discoveries. However, expansion into the area had been slow, largely due to the anti-wizard sentiment prevalent among the witches of the Catsi Crossing region.

The guilds offered a politically neutral foothold, allowing wizardry to re-enter the region – though not without strict oversight from local authorities. Wizards were expected to adhere to the guidance of local wisdoms and their spirits, some of whom were more tolerant of Foxfire’s presence than others. Nevertheless, a powerful and mutually beneficial collaboration began to take root.

The administration of the Zauberei guilds is, unofficially, seen as an extension of Foxfire Academy itself – with the exception of a few fiercely independent branches that reject affiliation with the institution. Recruitment often favors academy-trained wizards, and non-magical fighters are generally seen as less desirable. Prospective members (even non-magical ones) are typically expected to pass an assessment in arcane knowledge and present a portfolio and/or reference demonstrating prior research or fieldwork experience.

Additional duties include, but are not limited to: Study and documentation of magical peculiarities (creature behavior, artefacts, flora/fauna, environmental anomalies), retrieval and safe containment of cursed or otherwise volatile magical items, arcane cartography and analysis of leyline shifts or arcane turbulences, acting as magical consults to other guilds or regional authorities during crisis.
