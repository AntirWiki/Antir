# History
## The Lord of Gilded Leaves
In the 973 BR, the archfae was discovered by [[Ninyva O'Niamh]] near the village of [[Marigold]]. The exact relationship between the him and the people of the village is unclear, but he was known to and revered by the village Wisdom, Penelope.

He was known at this time as the Lord of Gilded Leaves, and was primarily a spirit of plants - particularly flowers, which filled his domain in the [[Tir na Nog#The Faewild|Faewild]].

When encountered by Ninyva, he agreed to rewrite the spellbook of the [[Lich|lich]] [[Kyrillos]], in exchange for the lich's soul - housed inside the book, which was his reliquary. He changed the book of necromancy to one of plant-inspired soul magic, which was later used extensively by [[Cianan of Antir]].

Later that year, he granted Ninyva incredible power in exchange for a portion of her soul, and an agreement that she would spread his influence.

## Kyrillos's Influence
> [!quote] From "The Hærfest Affairs"
> Kyrillos, Master of Whispers, was an expert in seizing opportunities. If no way forward was presented to him, he’d gladly open doors for himself by reaching through others to turn the handle. One does not become a Lich without the perfect storm of combination of a wisened scholar’s immense drive to push onward, and a failed, ignorant youth’s complete disregard for the consequences of your actions on other people. Unfortunately, it was that same coincidental gale of traits that led him into the ambitious hands of Ninyva O’Niamh, and later the impulsive, short-sighted council of the Lord of Gilded Leaves.
Now this was a door that was _begging_ to be opened. Ripe with delicious fear and crippling insecurity. The Lich had no physical form after being drawn out of the book, but was trapped in the Lord’s vicinity all the same. A relentless itching at the back of the mind. A consequence the Lord had taken willingly, both to protect his new pylon into mortal dealings, and to attempt to satiate that gnawing curiosity that befall all fey that witness the cycle of the seasons enough to start to find boredom in it. The Lord couldn’t help but eventually break the seal and start conversation between them. All Kyrillos had to do was offer a single support pillar to this crumbling tower, and the Lord of Gilded Leaves, a powerful archfey and ancient spirit, fell into the trap of an almost-mortal hubris, and tumbled straight into the Lich’s waiting hands.
Years of subtle dealings under the guise of tutelage. A hand of extended collaboration that tied puppet strings around trusting fingers. Promises of repairing his reputation in the Court after the victory of Cianan, and even providing a way to cull mortals who had grown too wise and strong against the chaos of the seasons and had begun to disregard their power. Delusions of grandeur were so much easier to produce in those that had already had a taste of both power and the loss of it. A tenuous waiting game played against the clock of his essence slowly dissipating into the feywild without a true phylactery or consenting anchor, but oh how he played it. This went beyond the capabilities of Lich-hood. If he played his cards right, he could become a new god. Kyrillos vowed to visit the grave of Ninyva some day to feed upon her soul in a twisted gratitude for the opportunity she had given him.
Maintaining facades. A benevolent lord, generous with gifting his blessings upon followers. The occasional Druid under his banner coming to the Major Court to complain… hearing the relentless vengeful whispers of plants and animals that had unfortunately passed within their training as healers of the land. They were dismissed and told to make peace with their failures. Eventually, the final knot was tied, and the question could be asked without fear of even subconscious hesitation. 
_“Make me a body.”_ 
And so it was done.
