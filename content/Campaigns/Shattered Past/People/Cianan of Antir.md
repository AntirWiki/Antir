Cianan of Antir (pronounced Kee-nun) was the king of [[Antir]] from 953-960 BR. He is known for being the victor of the [[Three Kings War]] against his brothers [[Faolan of Antir|Faolan]] and [[Lasairian of Antir|Lasairian]]. 

He is also seen as the founder of modern wizardry thanks to his use of battlemages during the war and his founding of [[Foxfire Academy]] in [[Zauberei]], where he would become headmaster after abdicating the throne to his younger sister [[Aisling of Antir|Aisling]].

After his abdication, he married his childhood friend and former royal guard [[West Hemlocke]].

He was the patron of [[Tooth and Claw]] immediately following the Three Kings War, and assisted them in defeating the dragon [[Dilitrius]]. He also helped fend off [[Ninyva O'Niamh]]'s attack on [[Alturon]] immediately following the dragon's assault.

# Description
Cianan is a tall, slender man with pale skin, long dark hair, and sharp features. Due to his elven ancestry he has slightly pointed ears and unusually large golden eyes.

# Abilities
Cianan is a master of animancy, the magic of soul manipulation. He is able to cast his own soul temporarily from his body, giving him control of specially made puppets. He is also able to manipulate the souls of others, causing confusion, altered motor and sensory function, or spiritual damage.

The spells he learned from [[Kyrillos]]' tome, which had been altered by [[The Autumn King]], let his soul regrow missing parts. This allowed him to safely split his soul and implant parts in others, where those parts would grow and grant him control over that person. He only used this ability to its full potential on his husband, Hemlocke, and briefly on the [[dragon]] [[Dilitrius]] during the Siege of Alturon.

Prophets of [[The Veil]] have seen visions of a being known as the Phantom King that had spiritual control over the entire population of Antir. Striking similarities have been noted between this creature's abilities and those of King Cianan.

# History
## Pre-War
Before his father's death Cianan lived a relatively sheltered life. His early love of learning and fascination with magic led him to be apprenticed to [[Zella Brightflower]], who was known in noble circles as a talented mage and effective teacher. While at her estate in Zauberei he met Hemlocke, and the two became close friends during Cianan's apprenticeship. When Cianan had to return to the capital, Hemlocke accompanied him as his valet.

Cianan became very fond of Zauberei during this time, and commissioned the construction of Foxfire Tower as his personal estate and study.

## The Three Kings War
After Tarlach's funeral, Cianan and Hemlocke were forced to flee Alturon to avoid the brewing conflict between Lasairian and Faolan. The pair evaded capture by Faolan's soldiers and made it to Zauberei, where they were able to remain thanks to Cianan's reputation in the city (and Zella's protection).

During the first year of the war, Cianan was approached by nobles and merchants seeking to back a third side in the conflict. He ultimately decided that neither of his brothers were fit to rule, and began collecting power to join the war.

He was initially dismissed by both sides, until he demonstrated the fearsome power of trained battlemages. He converted Foxfire Tower into a military training academy, and lines of low-level mages introduced a variable to the battlefield that no generals of the time were prepared to counter.

Despite this advantage, he held significantly less troops and territory than Faolan and was slowly losing ground when Lasairian allied with the dragons. In the face of this new threat, Cianan allied with Faolan and the two spent the latter half of the war defeating Lasairian. In the final battle of the war, when Faolan killed the draconically-transformed Lasairian and was himself wounded, Cianan's mages demonstrated devastating effectiveness by using mass castings of low-level earthshaping magic to collapse entire canyons on enemy formations.

Cianan took advantage of Faolan's wounded state after the battle, using soul magic - his arcane specialty - to sever the wounded king's soul from his body, causing him to slowly die of apparently natural causes.

## Reign
The first year of Cianan's reign was fraught with conflict. His "victory by default" in the war made many question his legitimacy as king, and Faolan's allies in Alturon were less receptive to Cianan.

He was also confronted with [[General Tanner|remnants of Lasairian's army]] causing chaos in the country, which he employed [[Tooth and Claw]] to deal with. This culminated in the Siege of Alturon in 953 BR, where he helped the adventurers defeat [[Dilitrius]] and briefly took control of the dragon. 

This turned Tooth and Claw against him, and led [[Ninyva O'Niamh]] to bargain with [[The Autumn King]] for the power to defeat him. When she attacked Alturon, he was able to briefly trap her in a mental projection where he, Hemlocke, [[Valerie Bouchard]], and [[Darios O'Mael]] were able to defeat her.

These disasters further weakened Cianan's reputation, causing him to eventually abdicate the throne to his younger sister Aisling in hopes of repairing the royal image.

After the Siege of Alturon, Cianan entered a romantic relationship with Hemlocke. The two married shortly before Cianan's abdication, briefly giving Hemlocke the title of Royal Consort. 

## Foxfire Academy
After his abdication, Cianan retired for Foxfire Academy, which by this point was being converted from a military institution to an academic one. He took over the duties of headmaster from Zella Brightflower, and with Hemlocke's assistance led the academy until his "death".

## Retirement
During his life, Cianan demonstrated the ability to cast his soul into inanimate vessels to control them. This, alongside his husband Hemlocke's fondness for woodcarving, leads many to believe that his soul entered such a vessel upon his death. The magical bond between the couple's souls may have also allowed Cianan to perform a similar magic on Hemlocke when he died.

Indeed, many around Zauberei - and all across Antir - have reported sightings of what appear to be a pair of animated wooden puppets, sometimes covered in illusory disguises.
