West Hemlocke was the assistant headmaster of [[Foxfire Academy]] from 965-989 BR. He was the bodyguard, sole confidant, and eventually husband of King [[Cianan of Antir]]. He was a member of the adventuring group [[Tooth and Claw]]. 

Despite Cianan's tarnished reputation as king, the duo had a formidable partnership, with Cianan's magical prowess complementing Hemlocke's physical strength to quell most political unrest. However, their reign remained unpopular among many, despite the pair's pivotal role in saving Alturon from the threat of the dragon [[Dilitrius]] in ~953 BR.

Historians generally recognize Hemlocke's critical part in securing Cianan's claim to the throne, and his essential support in ensuring [[Aisling of Antir]]'s succession following Cianan's abdication.

# History
## Early Life
In the autumn months of ~926 BR, Hemlocke was born -- or rather, hatched --  under the care of the esteemed wizard [[Zella Brightflower]]. Initially known as Hemlocke Brightflower in his youth, he was believed to be female until he began to exhibit traits typically associated with male puberty. This misunderstanding did not generally affect his upbringing, however, as Hemlocke was raised in a gender-neutral manner. 

The identity of his biological parents remains largely unknown -- except for the fact that his biological father was an elven druid and an old acquaintance of Zella, while his biological mother is presumed to be of [[Moondancer|owlfolk]] descent. 

At the age of 15, as traditional for gnomeish culture, he chose the first name West for himself. Generally, only family and people he considers himself close to he lets use this name.

From an early age, Hemlocke was taught through rigorous lessons to read and write by his mother, who faithfully adhered to the Gnomeish value of learning and experience. Zella treated him akin to an apprentice, and her authoritarian parenting style caused strain in their relationship, though this education allowed Hemlocke to surpass than the average Antirian in intelligence.

Despite his mother's best efforts, Hemlocke was unable to do magic for reasons Zella was never able to discover. This inability instilling into adulthood a desperate desire for approval despite his shortcomings and a perfectionist attitude.

She could manage the size difference for the most part with magic, but it could only do so much. He was frequently 'babysat' by Zella's various apprentices over the years, as he quickly outgrew his 3 foot mother within his first year.

Aside from the apprentices, who were generally older than him, Hemlocke did not get very much interaction with people his age. This, combined with Zella's straight forwarded communication style, caused Hemlocke to develop poor social skills, preferring the forests around his home to human interaction. He was often understood best by wizards -- those who understood the laws of the universe in a way deeper than just words. He wasn't completely ignorant of proper mannerisms, however; another teaching of his mother. His mother frequently attended upper-class meetings in Zauberei, and Hemlocke was expected to act the part. 

Their relationship wasn't all disdain, however. Indeed, Zella allowed the curious young boy to pursue his interests in whatever way he saw fit, from whittling, to poetry, to hunting, trapping, archery, horses, reading or exploring; sometimes paying experts in these areas to entertain and/or mentor the young Hemlocke. From these mentors, he discovered a deep love for hunting and the intricacies of the arcane. He would often trade his catches and their raw materials in town, and eventually found employment in Zauberei's Hunter's guild as an apprentice.

## Royal Employment
Hemlocke met Cianan during the mage-prince's apprenticeship to Zella, and the pair quickly became close. When Cianan's apprenticeship ended, Hemlocke accompanied him back to [[Alturon]] as the prince's steward. 

### Flight from Alturon
following the coup of [[Faolan of Antir]], Hemlocke assisted in the princes escape back to Zauberei. Historians regard this event as the beginning of Hemlocke's shift to a more martial role under Cianan's servitude. The pair was pursued by the royal guard for an unknown amount of time. Back in Alturon, common public belief was that Hemlocke had kidnapped the prince, and whether he was even alive was uncertain until Cianan entered the [[Three Kings War]].

## Later Life
In his later years, Hemlocke developed a deep passion for sculpting, even earning commissions from the royal family to create pieces for their collection. Among his works was a statue of Queen Aisling in Alturon. Though he was skilled with various materials, he seemed to favor wood the most.

In the eyes of the citizens of Zauberei, Hemlocke's hobby became a strange obsession. He crafted countless mannequins of his husband, each version more lifelike than the last—made from porcelain, marble, ivory, clay, and wood—each one inching closer to a perfect, unsettling replica, animating them after his death. Some of his works are rumored to still be locked away in the depths of Foxfire towers, to never see the light of day again. 

Hemlocke aided in running [[Foxfire Academy]], even after Cianan's death. Following Cianan's death, he retired to the Brightflower Estates, occasionally accepting apprentices wishing to learn sculpting, physical combat, or the magi arts (spell blading) -- if the apprentice can tolerate the dozens of strange puppet servants he utilizes, his favorite being a near-identical replica of his late husband, giving him a reputation among Zauberei as sad, peculiar, and unable to let go.

	- He is able to use his beak to tear apart things, if necessary, though.
