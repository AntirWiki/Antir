Ninyva "Nini" O'Niamh is the former Wisdom of [[Catsi Crossing]], [[Witch|Witch]] of [[The Catsi]], founder of the [[Catsi Coven]], and former member of [[Tooth and Claw]].

# General Nini Backstory

Ninyva O’Niamh was born 16 years before the 3 Kings War started. At the age of 10, she began apprenticing under the Wisdom of Catsi Crossing, Siobahn O’Breislein. At the time, Siobahn was 46. Note, in Catsi Crossing, and many other villages in central Antir, it is customary that the apprentice to the village Wisdom typically serves as apprentice until after she has reached the age of 40, she has raised a child, or the previous Wisdom dies, whichever happens first. 

Catsi Crossing was located near to a well trafficked bridge. The villagers were very used to travelers. So, when [[Faolan of Antir|the Wolf]]’s army came to town during the first year of the war, the town was apprehensive if not surprised. The Wolf, in his ambition and arrogance, insisted that Catsi Crossing and every other village in the area swear fealty to him and support him in the war effort. Siobahn, as a leader of the village and ever the stalwart, stubborn curmudgeon, all but kicked the prince and his army out, informing that Catsi Crossing has and always will be independent of “the messy chaos of monarchal life”. 

The prince was slighted, and **Yvonne Silvershield**, captain of his guard, exacted revenge on the village. Catsi Crossing was put to the torch, on the order of either the Wolf himself or some sycophant of his. Most of the villagers were okay, but one of the main casualties was Wisdom Siobahn O’Breslein. With the woman’s death at the age of 52, Nini, aged 16, was the only person capable of taking over the role of village Wisdom.

To call Nini a novice witch at this point would have been an understatement. Much of an apprentice wisdom’s life is spent preparing to take over the magic of the Catsi, the guardian spirit of Catsi Crossing. At this point in Nini’s life, she would not have even begun learning about the intricacies of magic. Thus began the next 6 years of Nini’s life, trying with all her might to learn naturecraft, witchcraft, and spiritcraft, all the while still having to attend to her many duties as Wisdom. 

Catsi Crossing never quite recovered from the Wolf’s scorching. Bandits were a real problem, especially now that the village was without its guardian witch. As Nini became more comfortable with her powers, these problems subsided, but the village still couldn’t recover. Crops didn’t come back, livestock couldn’t flourish as it once did, and everyone was kinda bummed. The village’s saving grace, ironically, came with a marching wizard battalion flying the flag of [[Cianan of Antir|the Fox]]. The future king had received a poisonous arrow to the gut courtesy of the Drake, and in all his vulpine wisdom, did not include first aid in his wizard army. Nini, taking advantage of the opportunity, made an agreement with the dying king: she would heal him to full fighting spirit. In exchange, the Fox would do everything in his power, should he become king, to house, protect, and make new lives for Nini’s people. The deal was struck, the king returned to the battlefield, and the rest is history.

# Nini in 953

After the war, Ninyva O’Niamh, 117th Wisdom of Catsi Crossing, and the other 1000 or so villagers made the long journey to the capital from the remnants of Catsi Crossing. The villagers, primarily farmers, fisherman, and riverboat operators, struggle to find their new place in the city, many of these skills not quite translating to urban life. Most of the men take on grunt work on river docks, doing hard labor where it’s needed. The people of Catsi Crossing are anxious and often turn to their Wisdom for what often can be trivial concerns. 

Nini, for her part, is a walking ball of stress. She has taken to working as an apothecary, though that doesn’t provide much. She struggles to know her role in this new environment, as the needs of the village Wisdom seem to be covered in large part by the resources of a city. Her main task for her people often ends up being to bully the king whenever he does something counterproductive to what she’s doing for her people. The guards of the castle know her by name at this point. 

Aside from simply navigating day to day life, Nini has her own secret dream. Wizardry, as she understands it, is an abomination, in anathema to everything she stands for. To Nini, it is a bastardization of the primal power of the spiritual world to use magic to your own personal needs. Magic is a lifestyle, magic is in every fiber of your being and in every moment of your life. To simply read a book and cause death at the scale exhibited during the Three Kings war is a blight upon the natural world and will only lead to the balance of the spiritual and mortal to collapse. This is Nini’s view on the matter, at least. To counter this, she wants to bring the witches of Antir together. If they can have cohesion, they can hopefully at least counter the inevitable expansion of wizardry outside of the army and spread the “truth” of the magical world.

For clarity, should things go poorly in this philosophical war between wizards and witches towards the end of the campaign, Nini doesn’t know it yet, but she fully intends to kill the king to curtail the spread of wizardry. 

# The Time Between

Some time after the Wolf and his army left, Nini, still a level 0 Witch, had an entirely new problem appear at her doorstep. A bandit troupe, led by an ogre named Ugurk the Pungent, descended on the village from the mountains. Ugurk was a shriveled, foul-smelling husk of an Ogre, but an ogre nonetheless, and he fancied himself something of a king. He and his mountain bandits took over the village by force, met with little resistance from the weakened, witchless villagers. Catsi Crossing essentially lived under occupation from this group for two years, despite outnumbering the bandits nearly 10-1. 

Ugurk was far from a benevolent leader. Early attempts at resistance were put down hard. People that tried to fight back died. This led to a sort of peace, with Ugurk’s bandits bullying whoever crossed them on any given day, but people otherwise staying safe. Nini, for her part, was consumed with shame and rage. 17 years old and she viewed what was happening as the greatest failure of any Wisdom in the history of Catsi Crossing, maybe even Antir. Though this wasn’t her fault, she took full responsibility. She knew that standing up to Ugurk in her current state would lead to her either dying or simply being mocked forever, both of which seemed equally terrible. Nini had to learn, become capable, and get her people in a similar mindsight. By day, Nini went about her duties as Wisdom, healing the sick, caring for babies, communing with the spirits of the forest, being all bows and curtsies for the occupying forces. By night, however, she steeled herself and her people. Nini had no knowledge of martial combat, other than what she had read in stories, but most of those involved fighting dragons, not other people. Nini read, studied, and practiced. The Catsi and her speed-ran their bond as witch and patron, cramming several years of a witch’s development into barely more than 1, while the villagers did what they could to hone their pitchfork and large stick prowess. Nini estimated that she never got more than 2 hours of sleep at a time throughout this entire period. 

Eventually, the night of the attack came. Ugurk had taken the mayor’s house just because it was the biggest building in town. The villagers fought the many bandits. Nini, for her part, played support, bolstering attacks, healing, dropping trees on people where she could. When it came to Ugurk, he had decided he would fight till his dying breath. In Nini’s own words, the fight ended when she “Horizon Thunder Sphered his arse” and left behind nothing but a burned skeleton. Nini then slept for 24 hours straight. The bandits that hadn’t been killed were driven out, never to return. Nini would continue to learn and watch over her village until the war ended. Things got worse, with the fields and livestock never recovering from Ugurk and the Wolf. See above for how this was resolved. Catsi Crossing now lays abandoned. 

At some point before Caenen appeared, an army of the Drake’s led by a man named Darios O’Mael appeared outside the village, whom Nini promptly and aggressively told to fuck all the way off.

# Notable Villagers

Nigel and Verin O’Niamh: Nigel and Verin are Nini’s parents. They were pig farmers that lived on the outskirts of Catsi Crossing. They were beyond excited and honored when their daughter had been chosen by Siobahn O’Breislein to be Wisdom’s Apprentice. They still regularly saw their daughter, since she had to travel around the town helping both on the farm and the villagers as fitting of her station. Both survived through Ugurk’s occupation. Tragically, Nigel succumbed to a pox that Nini was unable to cure when she was around 21 years old. Verin now lives in a small house in the capital with the family’s beloved pet pig, Hog (who Nini considers her favorite person in the whole world).

  

Mayor Patrick O’Raileigh: Patrick O’Raileigh, 38,  is the Mayor of Catsi Crossing, or whatever that looks like without Catsi Crossing existing anymore. He took over the role after the previous mayor, Maynard O’Callahan, was killed resisting Ugurk early in his occupation. Patrick primarily acts as a community leader for what is now just a neighborhood within a much larger settlement. In the village, Mayor and Wisdom were theoretically equal in power, with Mayor serving more to deal with the economic and political aspects of running a trading village. Historically, however, Mayors typically acted at the discretion of the reigning Wisdom. 

  

Connor O’Sullibahn: Connor O’Sulllibahn is Nini’s rat bastard of an ex boyfriend. Nini and Connor, a cobbler, got together after the occupation of Ugurk. They were together for a few years. Nini was quite fond of the man, and had simply assumed they would get married and all the things that typically go with that. Connor, however, made the baffling decision to cheat on the most powerful person in the village. Nini was heartbroken and conflicted. On the one hand, it went against both who she was and her job to cause bodily harm to one of her villagers, no matter how much she wanted to. On the other hand, Nini was nothing if not persistent and petty. So it simply became a topic of conversation with every person she helped, for like a year. She made it everyone’s problem. Connor’s general vibe nowadays is embarrassed loner.
