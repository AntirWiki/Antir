Aisling of Antir (pronounced Osh-ling) was queen of the [[Lucentia|Holy Kingdom of Antir]] from 960 BR until her death in 1021 BR. She was also a paladin of [[Lumina]], and during her reign she brought that church under royal control.

# Description
As a youth, Aisling was a thin but strong girl with long blonde hair and a serious expression.

# History
In her youth, Aisling was mostly raised by her lady-in-waiting, an older woman who Aisling saw as a mother. The woman was a devout follower of Lucent, and the teachings she shared with Aisling inspired the girl's devout faith.

## Reign
In the formative years of Aisling's life, all that she knew was strife. Her brothers went to war for seemingly no reason for nearly all of her childhood, followed by a year of political instability, which ended in no less than two existential threats to her kingdom. In her eventual ascension to the throne of Antir, she refuses to make the same mistakes as her brothers. She turns to her goddess for guidance, and in this comes to a realization. The priests would seek to use her. The nobility would seek to use her. Even the merchants and lesser houses would seek to use her. But, she was above that. Through Lucent, she was above all of the petty squabbles of mortals. In the ensuing decades, Antir would take a fairly radical change to its approach to governance. Conferring with the Lady of Light in all things, Queen Aisling of Antir would take a much more authoritarian approach in her reign. She brings the church fully under the command of the throne, with herself acting as the direct conduit between the people and Lucent. The houses were told in no uncertain terms that they served the kingdom and God, and not the other way around. While this wasn't necessary an entirely peaceful transition, Aisling's lasting legacy would be the founding of the Holy Kingdom of Antir, with herself as the first theocrat of this new land.
