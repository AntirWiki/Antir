Warn the players - this is session zero, so it might get a bit railroady. Your choices do matter, but there are certain things that need to happen.

On that note, request that people keep an eye on their DMs. Tell cas "Help Juniper when they get stuck, you'll know what I mean when you get there"

# Blight
While in the woods, a Blight check must occasionally be made. The first time this happens, it is a DC 10 fortitude save. On a failure, the character becomes Drained 1. Each subsequent test has a DC 5 higher, and increases drained value by one.

# Introduction
> [!recite] Elmhollow
> The year is 1362, near the end of a long summer, and we find ourselves in the town of Elmhollow, in the west of  [[Strioc an Athraithe]]. It's a respectable town, but not especially large - you may not know everyone's names, but those of you who live here probably recognize everyone's face.
> 
> Thistle, today you found yourself at the home of Madam Verelle, an old spinstress. After an hour or two of chores and a nice lunch, she gave you a strangely sad look and set you loose. "It's a nice day, and winter will be here soon. Go play, make some friends, be a kid while you still can," she said as she shooed you outside.
> 
> You know that Madam Verelle will hear about it if you don't at least *try* to follow her advice, so you go looking. As fortune would have it, you soon find the perfect scene.
> 
> Two red-haired humans, around your age, are standing in a yard and seem to be starting some kind of game. The younger one, a girl, is holding a cat. Cas, can you describe what Willow looks like at this time of her life?
> 
>The older, with shorter hair, is her sibling Juniper. They’re wearing simple clothes, and are holding a wooden sword. You three at least know *of* each other, even if you haven't interacted much. There's not that many kids in town, especially so close to your own age. Thistle, you know these two are the priests' children, and Willow, you'd know that Thistle is the odd orphan who hangs out with the local witch, Leskella. You may have interacted in the past, that’s up to you to decide. 
>
> Thistle, you also notice the massive figure leaning against a building nearby, watching over them. Rock, you've been asked to keep an eye on the Beaumont kids for today. Their parents have given you a lot of support lately, so you accepted the chance to return the favor a bit. How does Rock look? 
> 
> Thistle, you've probably heard that there's a Minotaur in town - they visit occasionally to trade - but you probably haven't seen Rock around much. Willow, you've seen Rock plenty - your father has been helping him out, and one of the church members even cleared out a barn for him to stay in. You've been told that Rock had a difficult time at his home, and he needs a bit of help getting back on his feet.
>
>Thistle, this seems like the best opportunity you're gonna get to "play" or "make friends" enough to satisfy Madam Varelle. How would they approach?
>
>Cas, would Willow say anything right away? If not, Juniper turns as they approach with an intensely curious stare.
>
>"You're Thistle, right? Do you wanna join us? We're trying to play adventurers, but we can't decide who's gonna be the monster."

Just as the game is about to begin, Waxwing suddenly stiffens and leaps from Willow's arms. The cat runs off towards the treeline on the west side of town, stops, looks back at the group, then darts into the bushes.

Either those children are about to be in danger, or there's something *very* suspicious going on. It'd be *negligence* to just ignore something like that, wouldn't it? You should probably follow them to find out what's going on.

# Into the Woods
>[!recite] The Forest
>You all have lived in or around Athraithe long enough to know that distance is... inconsistent, here. Sometimes you can walk west for hours before the trees start turning gray - sometimes you can even find fruit this way, in late summer months like these.
>
>Today, you reach the edge of the Silent Wood in minutes. The trees become withered and gnarled before you even have a chance to think about turning back. You periodically catch a glimpse of calico coat ahead of you, just often enough to know that you're still on Waxwing's trail. You've been chasing her due west, and it doesn't look like she's going to change course. 

The woods are dark, and there's clearly something following them - a large dark shadow flits between the trees in the corner of one's eye.

After a bit of walking, all players must make a Blight save (DC 10)

Eventually, they come upon a strange mushroom circle. If they step into it, the fight starts with surprise - otherwise, the myconid elder tries to sneak up on them - but the group are alerted by a loud, uncanny raven call.

(Two **follower of Shumfallow**, two **Hogweed Leshy**, two **Plum Leshy** and a **weak Weakened Abyssal Fungi**. The Plum Leshy's virulent burst effect is replaced by the effect of their Bitter Harvest. Burst effects can only be activated while the Abyssal Fungi lives. The blue mushrooms can only be hurt by bludgeoning and lightning damage, the red only by slashing and fire, the green only by piercing and poison. Each have one HP.)

During the fight, one of the creatures goes for Thistle and is eliminated by a large dark shadow that swiftly flies down from the trees, grabs the mushroom, and flies off.

It's clear that the tallest one was once a person - the fungus is grown over a skeleton, still wearing strange jewelry.

# Turned Around
The party quickly realize that they've lost their orientation during the fight. A skill challenge ensues to find their trail - either by tracking the cat, identifying their heading, or something else. Each time a check is failed, the Blight check must be rolled by the entire party. 

>[!recite] The Altar
>Soon after you resume your chase, you stumble upon another small clearing.
>
>The first thing you notice are the skeletons. Very similar to the one that just attacked you, they have moss and fungi growing all over their yellowed bones. Both are kneeling before what looks like a huge rotten stump with an unusually flat surface. 
>
>Looking closer, there's an indentation in the stump. It looks scorched, as if something was burnt within it, and there's burnt remnants of some spongy white material. Around the divot are lines of very fine ash... Willow, you recognize it as something like burnt incense.

It's identifiable from the surroundings that despite the skeletons' advanced decay, they have only been here for a few days at most. There's also an empty spot, presumably once filled by the being that laid the mushroom circle.

Rock and Willow are able to easily recognize that this is the remains of some religious rite. The offering is laid out not like one to a fae, but like one to a god - you don't burn offerings to a fae. Hags don't usually worship gods, except the Witch Queen - but a high enough religion or occultism check knows that her offerings are usually flowers and herbs, not mushrooms.

# The Tree
The players continue walking, and must make another Blight save.

The party finds themselves in a much much larger clearing, with a massive twisted oak in the center. The other trees leave it a wide berth, as if afraid to be touched by its reaching, spiked branches with ends like clawed hands.

Juniper sees the cat nestled in a hollow of the tree's roots, and rushes forward - completely missing the corpses of people and animals that rise up around them. (Six **Zombie Shambler**s with reduced HP - 12 each)

The enemies block anyone but Willow and Juniper from approaching the tree. Juniper rushes to the cat, but as they step forward to grab it, are suddenly caught in the tree's roots.

When Willow grabs their hand to help them free, she feels a chill travel up her arm and through her body. In that moment, everyone must make another Blight save. Juniper cries out, and falls unconscious - though as they fall, they're no longer stuck at all.

At this point the undead stop strategizing, instead just attacking mindlessly. More and more arise until the party grabs Juniper, Willow grabs the cat, and they flee.

# End of the Beginning
The group makes their way out of the forest unimpeded. Shaken by the experience, where does each person go?

Juniper goes with Willow unless she stops them, uncharacteristically quiet and complaining of a headache
