Juniper suggests that they all gather for a meal, and asks Willow to help gather everyone. 

Each person gets to describe where they are and what they're doing when they're collected.

Once they're all gathered, Juniper remembers that they offered to help Farmer Wurm on the edge of town take care of his pigs today. Asks the party if they'll help, since "many hands make light work". 

When the party arrives at the farm, on the eastern edge of town, Farmer Wurm is irate at Juniper being "late", and in his anger, accidentally looses the pigs. The party must somehow herd a dozen enthusiastic pigs back into their pen.

Don't set Juniper up on the map during pig-herding. When the party finishes, they're nowhere to be seen - but Wurm insists that the group help him take care of the pigs. It takes almost an hour, and by the time they finish around sunset, they can see large amounts of smoke rising from the town square.

On their way to the square, they find a terrible sight. Two villagers - once bakers, people the party knew - are hunting down other townsfolk. They're feral and snarling, having been raised by Kyrillos as Ghasts.

# Esquire Aralia of the Grasping Vine
The party are halted on their course by a tangle of vines. Before them stands a tall figure - almost seven feet - made entirely of vines, holding a sword made of some dark metal.

"Halt, minions of foul Autumn! You shall no longer trespass in my domain! Surrender yourselves to me, Esquire Aralia of the Grasping Vine, and I may have some small mercy upon you."

The spirit rolls a +3 deception check to fool the party - because they are, in fact, a much smaller creature stacked atop two others in the vague shape of a person. A successful Nature check will also reveal that Esquire is a laughably low rank - in fact, Aralia is specifically the ruler of "vines that trip people".

They believe that the party are minions of Juniper. If convinced otherwise, they offer the party the cold iron sword they wield ("we can barely hold it, it's freezing hot") and a Stone of Encouragement.

If the party fail to convince them or attack, they fight alongside two others that were disguised as vines on each flank (a total of five **Weak Vine Leshy**)
