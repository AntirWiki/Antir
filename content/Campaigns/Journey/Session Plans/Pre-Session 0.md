# Intro
The town of [Elmhollow](https://antirwiki.github.io/Antir/Strioc-an-Athraithe/Locations/Elmhollow) lies in northwestern Athraithe.  Known locally as a haven for unusual folk, it sees all manner of visitors - both witches and wizards have been known to pass through, and even the secluded Minotaur visit to trade.

Years ago, you and a few others left Elmhollow and ventured into the nearby Silent Woods. You returned from the shadow of that wood intact, but not unchanged. Now, something emerges from those dark trees to threaten all of Antir, and only the five of you can stop it. To do so, you'll have to journey across the entire continent - and hope that by the end, you're strong enough to survive.

(What happened the first time the group went into the Wood? We'll be finding that out during a one-shot/mini-campaign set several years before the main game.)

# Polls
It's finally almost time to start making characters and planning this campaign, but first I want to set some baselines.

These polls are for me to gauge the themes and tone everyone wants for this campaign. Every poll will be multivoting: vote for as many options as you want (you're not limited to one vote per poll), and if you have more nuanced opinions, feel free to share in # game-text.

Now, onto some logistics questions:

- Character creation has an option called "free archetype" where you get to take a bonus feat tree (including things like weapon specializations, multiclassing, crafting stuff, beast taming, all kinds of options. Like, a LOT of options.) It makes your character stronger overall and can lend some nice thematic stuff, but there's a TON of options so it makes characters more complicated. Would we like to use it? (There's no balance issues here - if we use it I'll just make enemies a bit stronger to compensate.)
	- Yes. More complicated character creation, but more powerful and thematic.
	- No. Regular character creation. Simpler and easier to keep track of.

I am currently envisioning this as a 12-18 month campaign, that goes from level 1 to 12. How does everyone feel about that?

If there is anything you want to see during this campaign - themes, scenes, moments, characters - please let me know! (Like last campaign, when y'all told me you wanted a masquerade)
