Arriving at [[Foxfire Academy]], they see an avian man giving instructions to a [[Doomed Party|diverse group]]: an amurran with a floppy wizard hat, a halfling man in dark leather armor, and two dwarves that look like sisters - one in plate mail with a greatsword and the other in gray robes, proudly wearing a holy symbol you don’t recognize. 

>[!aside] 
>“If I may offer a tactical suggestion: these tunnels are a la-” he glances at Rock, “a maze, and full of hiding places. I suggest you ensure they are cleared before you confront the being controlling these undead, lest it recalls any scouts or stragglers to attack you from behind.”

The party meet [[General Konketsu]] and are given a map of the crypts. 

# Dungeon
![[basementmap.png]]
- 4-5 **Shadow Wisps** that hide in the darkness and intentionally snuff out any lights that appear during the fight.
- An **Elite Luminous Ooze** that has been loosed from a containment tank and will chase the players as soon as it is discovered. The tank is still intact, and if the players can lure it in and close the door it will be once again trapped.

- A guard - human woman - has been sacrificed to the Autumn King, but she yet lives. There is fungus crawling its way through her body, which can be extracted with a DC 18 Medicine check or the ritual halted with a DC 18 Religion check. If she is not saved, Silas gains an additional second rank spell during his fight. 
	- She is held in place by a set of Average manacles

- Mages that are hiding and will attempt to attack the players at first sight, thinking they are among the attackers.

- A guardian enchantment on the wall leading up to the sealed door, with the effects of [Entombed Spirit](https://2e.aonprd.com/Hazards.aspx?ID=151).

Boss: Willow's father, risen from the dead as a **Deathless Acolyte** of the Autumn King. Accompanied by two Foxfire guards that he has raised as **Void Zombies**.

He finds himself unable to harm Willow because of his deep-set fatherly instincts.

# Aftermath
The party return above to find the dwarven woman, plate mail scrapped and sword shattered, heavily wounded and crying. 

>[!recite] Abrail's Account
>“It moved so fast… it went right past me, took down my sister first, and without her healing we couldn’t last long enough. Emyr was nearly cut in two, Halic got caught by that green fire… eventually I couldn’t stand up anymore, and it just… ignored me. Like it didnt matter if I was alive or dead, as long as I was out of the way. I think we hurt it pretty good, maybe we could have won if we were smarter…”

### Archmage
The party are asked to stay nearby for a bit, as the Archmage will likely want to speak with them when she returns.

A few hours pass, the group having free reign of the academy and [[Zauberei City]].

Eventually they are summoned to the office of Archmage [[Irina Varazs]], at the top of Foxfire Tower. Arriving, they are waved in by her assistant, a quiet dwarven woman.

>[!recite] Entering the office
>The dwarven woman nods and gestures for you to enter. Through the door, you enter a room that can only be described as *arcane*. Every wall is covered in bookshelves, only about half of which contain books - the other half have an assortment of items unlike any you've ever seen. It's impossible to tell what's an instrument, artifact, tool, or just decor. There's an astrolabe with three slowly orbiting moons, a jade skull with a slowly pulsating light within it; there's a smoke-filled mirror on a stand, and as you glance at it, a pale hand emerges from the smoke, gently traces a single line on the glass, and vanishes again.
>
>The center of the room is taken up by a massive dark wood desk, even more cluttered than the bookshelves with tomes, papers, and knickknacks. Behind it, a tall half-orc woman watches you. She has greenish-tan skin and small tusks, with long dark hair flowing over her shoulders. Thistle, you would immediately recognize her as Archmage Irina Varazs, the most skilled Wizard in Foxfire - and possibly the world.
>
>There's a few chairs set out before the desk, but she does not wait for you to sit. "I'm told that you know about the thing that wrecked my library."

Irina asks for all the information they have on Juniper, and gives as much advice as she can. If the party imply that they're going after them, she warns them that Abrail's group were upper Silver rank, nearing gold. This means they were around level 9.
