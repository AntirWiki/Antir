# Hook
The city of [Dragun mac Tire](https://antirwiki.github.io/Antir/Strioc-an-Athraithe/Locations/Dragun-Mac-Tire), deep within the fae-warped woods of [Athraithe](https://antirwiki.github.io/Antir/Strioc-an-Athraithe/Strioc-an-Athraithe), is known for one thing above all else: adventurers. This is no accident; after all, it was built around the headquarters of the [Adventurer’s Guild of Antir](https://antirwiki.github.io/Antir/Miscellanious/Groups/Adventurer#the-adventurers-guild).

As a rookie adventurer yourself, you’ve made your way to the city in search of opportunity. Among the dozens of quest listings posted by the Guild, a particularly flashy one catches your eye. The pay is mediocre, but as a copper rank adventurer -especially a brand-new one - you have to take what you can get. 

Those new to Antir are welcome to read (or skim) the [Voyager’s Guide](https://antirwiki.github.io/Antir/Miscellanious/Voyager's-Guide-to-Antir), but it’s totally optional. I’m also always always happy to answer questions about Antir or about pathfinder.

# Mayspring Hares
>[!recite] Wumber Arrives
>As you all finish your introductions, one of the wagon doors swings open loudly. From it strides a gnome wearing the gaudiest outfit you have ever seen - tassels, sequins, and gems adorn the blue and gold outfit, which is capped with a shining golden top hat. The gnome has a wide smile under a mustache so long and pointed that it almost extends beyond the sides of his head.
>
>"Why hello there my friends, you must be the brave heroes I inquired about! Allow me to introduce myself - I am the illustrious Wumber Timble, the ringmaster of this incredible troupe, at your service - or, well, I suppose you're at mine, for the moment! Ha! What are your names, my noble friends?"
>
>"Well, it is truly *excellent* to meet all of you. It looks like we're almost ready to go, so sharpen those swords or whatever it is you people do when-"
>
>He's cut off by a loud, slow creaking noise from a wagon behind you. He looks past you all, where a wagon door is creaking open with agonizing slowness.
>
>The door isn't an ordinary one - it's made up of iron bars, and you see that the wagon it leads to is some sort of cage. A sign above it reads "Mayspring Hares", and sure enough, you see a furry head poke out from the now-open door. It looks like a hare, brown and black, though larger than you'd expect. Then, in a blur, it flies out of the opening. As it lands you see that it is no ordinary hare - its limbs are extended freakishly into a humanoid shape, and long claws extend from its front paws. Before you can react, several more of the creatures leap from the cage opening.
>
>Wumber stares at them, eyes wide. "Um. Your first task is to deal with that! Please don't harm them too much, but *get them back in the cage!* Good luck!" He steps backwards, into his wagon, and slams the door shut.

“Mayspring Hares” are *supposed* to be little yellow hares that float in the wind, but these creatures are hares freakishly extended to humanoid proportions

Predatory Rabbit

Objective: don’t kill, but scare/herd/carry them back behind the cage’s cold iron bars.
