# Player Info
>The College of the Occult has always been... *unique* among the schools of Foxfire Academy. Sequestered in its own campus, hidden in the forest near Zauberei City, the college is often looked down upon by the wizards of the other colleges. They see every subject taught by the occultists - necromancy, augury, dowsing, and more - as either foolish or dangerous - if not both.
> 
>You, of course, know that they're all fools. As a member of the college, you know the truth - that the forces of real magic cannot be described by a book, or taught in sanitized classrooms. They mock the shadowed rituals that you teach, but those rites produce results that would shake their very concept of reality. The traditions that they call strange are important steps to prepare the mind for deep arcane truths.
> 
>Needless to say, the deep hoods and billowing cloaks you wear are also very necessary. 
> 
>The Dean of the college is dead - though the body has yet to be found - and you have been nominated to take his place. But before you can, you and your fellow nominees must find out who killed the last dean - lest you become their next victim.

## Character Creation
Each of your characters is an important faculty member of the [[Foxfire Academy#College of the Occult]]. Perhaps a department head, an administrator, a teacher, or some other role.

Level 5 characters, any class, spellcasters are recommended.

# Introduction
>[!recite] Introduction
>You all stand in the atrium of Foxfire Academy's College of the Occult. It's a grand room, with dark stone walls and a vaulted ceiling. Despite it being midday, the room is shadowed - even the ever-burning torches in their sconces only serve to deepen the shadows in the corners.
>
>With you stands the woman who summoned you here. A shorter half-orc with dark hair cut at shoulder-length, she gives off an air of stark professionalism. You all know her - she's [[Miriam Varazs]], Headmaster of Foxfire Academy. Her sister [[Irina Varazs]] is the academy's Archmage, and the pair have been running the university for several years now.
>
>She brushes a bit of dust off her well-fitted amber robe. It's always a bit dusty in the atrium, since no cleaners are allowed into the college's inner sanctum. Miriam herself is the only outsider who's been here in years - it's usually off-limits to all but initiated occultists, but nowhere in the college is off-limits to the headmaster.
>
>She clears her throat as the last of you arrive. "Good, you're finally here. I'd like to get this over with as soon as possible."
>
>She gestures to the center of the room, where a shining silver statue sits. The plaque affixed to its base commemorates the late Dean of the college, Albert Terimo. 
>
>"I'm sure you know that your dean has been killed. Most of Zauberei knows, after that stupid Contingency of his went off. Just so you know," she points at the group, "if any of *you* write 'I have been killed' in the fucking sky, I will find a way to fine your corpse."
>
>"Anyways. The Occult needs a new Dean, and each of you have been nominated as a potential replacement. There's just one catch: nobody has found Terimo's body, and we don't know who did the guy in. As the ones who stand to gain the most from his death, you all are the top suspects. And because I don't want to appoint a murderer to upper administration, I'm not going to pick *any* of you until we know who killed him.
>
>"Which brings me to our current situation. I refuse to spend my time digging through this nest of scheming bastards you all live in, so I'm going to make you do it. If you don't find out who did it by the end of the week, none of you are getting the job - I'll bring in someone from the outside to take over. I'm sure you don't want to be led by an 'outsider', and I don't want to subject anyone else to your insanity, so for both our sakes, you'd better do some quick investigating."
