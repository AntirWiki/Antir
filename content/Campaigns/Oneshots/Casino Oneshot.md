# Objective
You've each been chosen for your unique skills. Perhaps you're a renowned gambler, a con artist, a bank robber. Whoever you are, you've been approached by one Lady Marietta Soleil, the femme fatale of the Holy Kingdom's finance world. She has a job for you: her husband owns the Golden Crane, the finest casino in Alturon. He hasn't emerged in weeks, and has been unreachable by means mundane or magical.

Your task: find him, and get him out. If Lord Soleil makes it out alive, you'll be handsomely rewarded. How you do so is up to you, but you'll get extra cash if you do it with discretion.

The Golden Crane is the richest game-house in the Kingdom, and winners emerge every day with lives changed. Roll the dice and play your cards well, and perhaps you'll join them. Or perhaps you'll join the rumored few who never make it out at all.

# Third Floor
>[!recite] Soleil's Office
>The moment you enter the office, you know that something is very wrong. The first thing you notice is that this room is *huge*; from the outside, the top floor couldn't have been more than thirty feet across. Now, you stand in a room that's at least twice that size. It's laid out less like an office, and more like the inner room of a temple - pillars line the walls, interspersed with braziers and ornate statues.
>
>A bit incongruously, there is a desk at the other end of the room. It's exactly what you'd expect to find in a casino owner's office; heavy, made of a dark wood, ornately carved. In the plush seat behind it sits a familiar looking man in a fine dark suit, who does not acknowledge your entry. The man - who must be Lord Soleil, based on the description you were given - is focused entirely on a dice cup in his hands, which you watch him pour onto the desk. He shakes his head, puts a few dice back in the cup, then rolls again. He keeps repeating this process over and over again as you take in the last person in the room.
>
>Standing a few feet in front of the desk is a tall avian figure. *Pause for Jenny to say Hemlocke*. They wear a shimmering golden robe over equally gold feathers on a thin, almost sticklike frame. Their long wings flutter slightly as they watch you enter.
>
>The Golden Crane addresses you. "Welcome to my sanctum, troublemakers."

**Fickle Fortune:** Each time a player character rolls a Natural One, the Crane's next attack automatically improves by one success level. Each time a player character rolls a Natural Twenty, the Crane's next attack automatically decreases by one success level. These effects can stack.
