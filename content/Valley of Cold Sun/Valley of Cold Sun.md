The Valley of the Cold Sun is an arctic region north of the [[Great Forest]]. The land is freezing cold, covered in near-constant snowfall.

The most notable - and dangerous - feature of the Valley is known as the "Cold Sun". Not only does the sun produce less heat than is typical in other regions, and shine unusually often for an arctic region, but any mortal who is exposed to the light is slowly driven mad.

The region is known to be home to massive reptilian creatures, whose motives and history are unknown.

At least one cult is known to be active in the region, worshipping mysterious winged figures who have control over frozen undead.

Some mortals live in tunnels under the Valley's mountains, potentially to avoid the maddening effect of the cold sun. However, generations of subterranean life have left them pale, malformed, and antisocial.

# Ecology

The Valley is the native home of [[Frostpetal]], which is said to have been grown by ancient residents as a balm for the Cold Sun's madness.

The valley is also home to the [[Tollan]], which burrow under the snow and capture unsuspecting surface dwellers.

# History
Ancient folk tales from [[Antir]] claim that Antirans were originally residents of the Valley before the Dawn of the Cold Sun drove them south into the Great Forest and eventually to the lands of the [[Dragon|Dragons]]. The oral histories passed down from this time imply that the unique solar effects were not always present in this region, and were perhaps caused by ancient mortal activity.

Many years later, after a war elsewhere on the planet, a group of refugees migrated to the Valley unknowing of its dangers. Over the course of a year they slowly fell victim to the region's madness and were mostly brought under the control of one of the frozen winged idols. The tunnel folk attempted to aid the refugees over the course of the year, but failures in communication led to violent conflict between the two groups. After one of the great reptiles attacked the settlement (possibly at the behest of the tunnel folk), it is unknown what became of the group.
