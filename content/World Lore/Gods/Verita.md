Verita is a goddess of light, truth, and purity worshipped mainly in [[Blackwing]]. She was once an aspect of the goddess Lucent until the schism between Verita and [[Lumina]] tore the goddess in two.

Verita is the patron goddess of Blackwing, and her church there has a significant role in the nation's government.

# History
## Lucent's Schism
[[Aisling of Antir|Queen Aisling]]'s connection to Lucent was born in a moment of desperation. In 953 BR, when her dearest friend was afflicted with lycanthropy - a curse that orthodox doctrine deemed a mark of evil requiring purification by fire - Aisling saw past centuries of dogma. Where others saw a beast to be purged, she witnessed a friend trapped between light and shadow. In her desperate prayer, Lucent revealed herself not as a harsh arbiter, but as a force of transformative mercy, bringing even those touched by darkness back to the light. Her Lucent was benevolent, warm, and kind - a goddess who could cure even those afflictions the Church condemned as unholy. This miracle shaped her reign, as she guided Antir towards a path of grace rather than persecution. 

But within the Church of Light, Bishop Felim saw this as heresy of the highest order. Once a humble priest from a land war-torned by the Three Kings War, he had witnessed his entire parish burned by heathens. His Lucent emerged from those flames - pure, untainted, and blinding. His Lucent does not heal, but razes heretics and smites unbelievers. In his eyes, Queen Aisling's "merciful" interpretation was nothing but a sickening weakness tainting divine truth. The goddess's words were absolute, not to be bent by mortal suffering. 

The schism festered like a wound in Antir's heart. Aisling's attempts at peaceful reformation - allowing common folk to read holy texts, distributing her priests to towns as healers - only fueled Felim's fury. His followers, the Pure Lights, began burning these new temples, calling them dens of heresy. In the harsh winter of 1020, when Queen Aisling lay weak and bedridden in her old age, Bishop Felim saw his divine sign. He declared the Purifying Crusade, rallying thousand of zealots who believed Aisling had corrupted Lucent's true nature.

It was then that the goddess herself shattered. Those who witnessed it speak of a sound like the breaking of the world itself - a divine fracture that split faith. From one force came two: Lumina, whose warm light brought healing and mercy, and Verita, whose searing radiance demanded purity through pain.

Felim's army, bearing Verita's burning standard, breached the walls of [[Alturon]] during the longest night of winter. They seemed unstoppable until they reached the royal chambers. There, in the darkness before dawn, they met their end at the moving hands in the night - the [[Keyshadows]], disciples of [[Rhylea]], moving like smoke through candlelight. Their daggers bore upturned key symbols, each death a locked secret never to be revealed. 

In the aftermath, Queen Aisling, perhaps guided by Lumina's mercy, did not seek vengeance. Instead, she granted the followers of Verita the right to practice their faith, though far from her seat of power. They retreated to the lands of Southeastern Antir, where they built white temples of marble and glass, each tower a finger of accusation pointed at the heavens. There, they await the day when truth's blinding light will scour the world clean.

To this day, pilgrims walking the road to these white cities speak of how the light feels different there - harsher, purer, like staring into the sun itself. And in the capital, healing temples still glow with a softer light, where Queen Aisling's legacy lives on in every gentle ray of dawn.

## Blackwing
During the [[The Godsbone War Chronicles|Dissolution]], when Blackwing was made an independent nation, High General Augustine Blackwing declared Verita to be their patron goddess and her priests their spiritual leaders.

Her role in the nation's governance was established in the nation's founding document, [[Blackwing#The Vultrite Precepts|The Vultrite Precepts]].
