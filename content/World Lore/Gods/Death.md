Death operates differently from other gods. Perhaps because all living things have a concept of death, it is not influenced by mortal minds the way other great spirits are. It manifests as a horse - or some other riding beast - in all of its appearances. For the past few centuries, it has also been accompanied by a [[Death and Her Rider|Rider]] - a figure in cavalier's armor who rides on its back or walks alongside it.

Because of its equestrian appearance, many funeral sites and cemeteries bear imagery of a horse's skull.

None have been able to identify where Death takes souls upon their passing. Souls that are resurrected or communed with often remember being ferried by Death, but in all cases are unable to describe where their souls went past that point. The memory may be erased from those crossing back into the living world, or the experience may simply be too alien for mortal language to describe.

Many faiths teach of an afterlife in Elysium, and claim that this lack of confirmation is a test of faith from the gods.
