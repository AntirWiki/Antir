Saints are spoken of in Antir with hushed whispers of equal awe and fear. The beings known as "saints" are uncanny fusions of spirit and mortal, sometimes also known as demigods. This fusion gives them powers beyond most mortal spellcasters, and more importantly, a near limitless potential for magical growth - so long as they have a source of spiritual energy (such as mortal faith or divine patronage). Saints are almost always physically and mentally altered by this double-existence, taking on strange forms and even stranger mentalities as their power grows.

The only known method of creating a saint is through divine intervention, by a process known as sanctification. Most gods will only grant sainthood to their most ardent clerics and champions, turning them into (what they see as) perfect vessels of faith and devotion.

Arcane experiments to replicate sanctification have only ever ended in tragedy. 

[[Dragon|Dragons]] are the first recorded examples of sainthood, created by holy rituals to the gods of the [[Drakos Empire]]. Given that the dragons eventually overthrew their gods and masters, they are seen as a precautionary tale against any faith over relying on the power of saints. 

The most famous Saint in Antir is the [[Golden Saint]], ruler of the [[Lucentia]].
