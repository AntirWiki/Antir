Zauberei is a city-state located in the northeastern corner of [[Antir]]. It is famous for its population of wizards and other spellcrafters, thanks to the magical school [[Foxfire Academy]] that is located in the center of the city.

It is also the primary trading destination for the [[Hallit]] of the northern and eastern mountains.

It shares its western border with [[Strioc an Athraithe]], and past the eastern mountains is the land of the [[Giant|Giants]].

# Locations
The majority of the country's trade and population are found in the capital, [[Zauberei City]].

Other notable locations include:
- [[Luscgard]], a small town south of Zauberei City.

# Government
Zauberei is governed by a council of five notable individuals (or representatives of those offices):
- Vox Populi, the elected city Mayor
	- Currently Mayor [[Tohko]]
- Vox Arcana, the headmaster of Foxfire Academy
	- Currently [[Miriam Varazs]], academy headmaster
- Vox Aegis, the general of the military
	- Currently [[General Konketsu]]
- Vox Argentum, representing the city's financial interests
	- Usually a powerful guild leader or merchant
- Vox Legatio, the city's head diplomat
	- Typically chosen by the previous Vox Legatio before their retirement

# History
Zauberei was founded early in the Great Kingdom era by gnome explorers led by one Zumbren Hopplepop. It was named based on the [[Hallit]] description of the area (*Zauber* being the Hallit word for magic).

During the Three Kings War, Zauberei served as the base of operations for [[Cianan of Antir]]. During this time, the wizard-king developed his personal tower into [[Foxfire Academy]] for the training of his army's famous battlemages.

After Cianan's abdication, he returned to Zauberei along with his husband [[West Hemlocke]] and the pair took over administration of the academy for the next few decades. 

In 1100 BR, the [[Lucentia|Holy Kingdom of Antir]] banned the use of magic within its borders. When royal inquisitors arrive before Zauberei, eager to finally discipline the heathens, they find themselves barred from entry. Then, they suddenly find themselves one hundred and fifty miles away from the city, with no memory of how they arrived there. Repeated attempts met similar resistance, each time depositing the royal agents at the exact same distance. This is how the borders of the city-state of Zauberei were first drawn, and marked their official independence from the Holy Kingdom of Antir.
