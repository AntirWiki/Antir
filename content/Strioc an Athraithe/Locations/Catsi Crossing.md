Catsi Crossing is a city-state located within [[Strioc an Athraithe]] in central [[Antir]]. It is the gathering place for all of Antir's witches and other primal casters, including the [[Catsi Coven]]. 

It is also the center of the fae energies that permeate Strioc an Athraithe. The impacts of the fae have even warped the architecture of Catsi Crossing, causing the buildings that already existed to bend and change into bizarre unnatural shapes, and causing many structures with seemingly no practical usage to appear. The long-term residents are also often subject to strange mutations.

The city is made up of colorful stone and wood buildings, windmills, and various buildings for coven activities.

# Locations
The **Hall of Synod**, one of the largest buildings in the town, is the meeting place of all Antir's witches during their decennial gatherings.

# Government
Catsi Crossing is led by the Catsi Coven, who meet in full once per decade in the town to direct the primal casters of Antir.

# History
## The Three Kings War
Catsi Crossing was located near to a well trafficked bridge. The villagers were very used to travelers. So, when [[Faolan of Antir|the Wolf]]’s army came to town during the first year of the [[Three Kings War]], the town was apprehensive if not surprised. The Wolf, in his ambition and arrogance, insisted that Catsi Crossing and every other village in the area swear fealty to him and support him in the war effort. Siobahn, as a leader of the village and ever the stalwart, stubborn curmudgeon, all but kicked the prince and his army out, informing that Catsi Crossing has and always will be independent of “the messy chaos of monarchal life”. 

The prince was slighted, and Yvonne Silvershield, captain of his guard, exacted revenge on the village. Catsi Crossing was put to the torch, on the order of either the Wolf himself or some sycophant of his. Most of the villagers were okay, but one of the main casualties was Wisdom Siobahn O’Breslein. With the woman’s death at the age of 52, [[Ninyva O'Niamh]], aged 16, was the only person capable of taking over the role of village Wisdom.

Catsi Crossing never quite recovered from the Wolf’s scorching. Bandits were a real problem, especially now that the village was without its guardian witch. As Nini became more comfortable with her powers, these problems subsided, but the village still couldn’t recover. Crops didn’t come back, livestock couldn’t flourish as it once did, and everyone was kinda bummed. The village’s saving grace, ironically, came with a marching wizard battalion flying the flag of [[Cianan of Antir|the Fox]]. The future king had received a poisonous arrow to the gut courtesy of the Drake, and in all his vulpine wisdom, did not include first aid in his wizard army. Nini, taking advantage of the opportunity, made an agreement with the dying king: she would heal him to full fighting spirit. In exchange, the Fox would do everything in his power, should he become king, to house, protect, and make new lives for Nini’s people. The deal was struck, the king returned to the battlefield, and the rest is history. 

After the war, Ninyva O’Niamh, 117th Wisdom of Catsi Crossing, and the other 1000 or so villagers made the long journey to the capital from the remnants of Catsi Crossing. The villagers, primarily farmers, fisherman, and riverboat operators, struggle to find their new place in the city, many of these skills not quite translating to urban life. Most of the men take on grunt work on river docks, doing hard labor where it’s needed. The people of Catsi Crossing are anxious and often turn to their Wisdom for what often can be trivial concerns. 

After Ninyva was defeated in Alturon by the other members of [[Tooth and Claw]] in 953 BR, [[Valerie Bouchard]] and [[Darios O'Mael]] buried her fae-corrupted heart in the ruins of the town. This may be the reason Catsi Crossing later became the center of the fae corruption in Strioc an Athraithe.

## The Catsi Coven
Six months after the Witch Queen's first death, in 954 BR, the formal inauguration of the Antir Witch Coven occurred. Taking place over the span of thirteen days, this gathering was one of the first great cultural exchanges in the history of the Antiran continent, with wisdoms and druids and alchemists and hedgemages from all across the land gathering the in the burnt remnants of Catsi Crossing in northern Antir. Ultimately, this premiere gathering accomplished a few things. Firstly, it established the purpose of the Coven: to protect the little people of Antir through the network of Wisdoms, and the protection of spirits and magic. Secondly, it established who was in charge. Though there wasn't an official leader of the Coven, very quickly one group gained influence throughout the group: three elder witches based out of the city of Zauberei, who held rather extreme views on what should be done to advance the group's goals. This trio would play a large part in outlining how the Wisdoms should operate, and would lead discussions in directions that they found desirable. Lastly, the Coven agreed to establish Catsi Crossing as the designated meeting place of the Coven, and that the witches would make the journey across the continent to the village once every ten years.

Reaching the end of their lives, the trio of Zaubereien witches fled the arcane powers of the wizard college and finally settled back in Catsi Crossing, along with a number of the more zealous members of the Coven. This group rebuilt the village, and established a home for magical individuals outside the confines of the clerics of Alturon or the wizards of Zauberei. The Zauberei trio would join the Spirits Beyond shortly after, leaving behind a legacy of radicalized witches and outreaching schemes.

Time continued to pass. As was agreed, the Antir Witch Coven continued to meet every 10 years in Catsi Crossing. Each Eldritch Assembly, more and more magical outcasts would join, including [[Hallit]] shamans from the north, and even a handful of dragon-seers from the far south. Many villages had begun to grow their ranks of magic users, with many expanding from one ruling individual to a large magical ruling council. Somewhere in this time, the term "wisdom" fell out of usage, with the term "witch" landing on the lips of both the witches themselves and their subjects. In this time, Catsi Crossing itself continued to grow, as casters outside of Alturon and Zauberei felt more and more without a home. The charred buildings and salted fields that met the first Eldritch Assembly had been replaced by stone buildings, alters to the Spirits Beyond and Below, and a great meeting hall designed to hold the many witches of Antir, though it felt more and more full each decade. These meetings continued in this manner. That is, until the Autumn King attacked.

When the Autumn King was banished, and autumn itself was erased, and magic itself was splintered into the primal, arcane, and divine domains, one final effect covered the land, unbeknownst to those who would be most impacted. Stretching from the village of Marigold, where the Autumn King once held court, to Catsi Crossing, where the Witch Queen's heart was buried, formed the Stríoc an Athraithe, a region where the barrier between the materium and Tir na Nog had been thinned.

Eventually news came of Alturon's magic ban. Following this, royal inquisitors appeared at the borders of Catsi Crossing, to purge the heresy of the witches. While Zauberei responded to a similar situation somewhat peacefully, redirecting the inquisitors away and seceding from the kingdom, the witches of Catsi Crossing, their bodies and minds heavily mutated from their extensive exposure to the Stríoc an Athraithe, responded in a different way. A few weeks later, Alturon received thirteen severed heads of the party they sent to Catsi Crossing with demands carved in both Sylvan and the Common Tongue into the faces of the heads. The message essentially contained three main points. First, Catsi Crossing and the surrounding territory were from that day forward separate from the Holy Kingdom of Antir. Secondly, the magic ban within the Holy Kingdom of Antir would _not_ apply to the witches of the villages and towns. They would be entirely exempt, and able to operate as they always have, maintaining their same relationship with Catsi Crossing that they have for the last 150 years. And third, if Alturon did not abide by these terms, they would face a peasant uprising from every village where Coven members operated, as well as outright war from the full eldritch power of Catsi Crossing and its allies. Initially, Alturon called the witches' bluff, and sent a band of soldiers to Catsi Crossing to take the leaders of the Coven. At the same time, envoys were sent to each village within 100 miles of the capital to capture their resident witches. When not a single one of these soldiers or envoys were ever seen again, Alturon abided by the Coven's terms. To the dismay of the Church and Throne, witches within Antir's borders could continue to practice their heresy, and Catsi Crossing would leave the Holy Kingdom. Zauberei would not be granted these same protections, and wizardry would remain outlawed within Antir.

Moving forward, the witches of Antir would continue to operate in this way. Catsi Crossing continues to exist as a sovereign country, though the effects of the Stríoc an Athraithe continue to warp and distort the people and creatures therein. The impacts of the fae have even warped the buildings of Catsi Crossing, causing the buildings that already existed to bend and change into bizarre, unnatural shapes, and caused many structures with seemingly no practical usage to appear. The witches of the country side still rule, as well as performing the historical duties of the wisdoms of old. These rural witches still bear the mutations of Catsi Crossing, though in a much less extreme form. Relations between Catsi Crossing, Zauberei, Alturon, and the other nations that have formed in the intervening centuries have not always been peaceful, however. Catsi Crossing has not forgotten the reason for its existence: the protection of the spirits of the land, and the destruction of wizardy. Zauberei remains the witch nation's enemy. Alturon still resents the treaty between itself and the Coven, and still works against it's existence, even if not through overt war.
