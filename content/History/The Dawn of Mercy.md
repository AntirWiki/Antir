***Excerpt from the Book of Light, Chapter Five***
*Translated from Celestial*

*The preceding chapter describes at length the virtue and faith of royal heir Áine of Antir during their early life.*

And so it was that in the year one thousand two hundred and seventy, the Great and Holy Kingdom of Antir was embroiled in a war within itself.

At this time the Noble Kingdom had been much diminished, for despite its unwavering holiness, it was led by mortal hands who had grown lax in their faith and their governance. Lands that were once united under Our Lady's grace were now fractured, and a mere hundred miles away from the Great City of Alturon, false kingdoms of heathens and usurpers obeyed not the guidance of the Church and Throne.

This fractured state was the cause of much unrest within the Great Kingdom. So it came to be that the Nephew of the rightful Queen of Antir was tempted by Foreign and Wicked powers, and turned upon her in open war.

This was a source of great turmoil for Áine. Though they doubted not the rightful place of their mother upon the throne, their traitorous cousin had been reared alongside them as if a brother. A paragon of Our Lady's kindness, Áine wished no harm to fall upon the misguided usurper. They held a great and true faith to the Light, so they prayed to Lumina, "Please, great Lady, show my cousin the error of his evil acts! Let my mother see your ways of mercy!"

Though their treacherous cousin tempted them and entreated them to betray their Mother, the faithful and pure Áine could not be swayed to Evil. And so, left astray without Lumina's blessing, the usurper turned to Heathen ways and left the Holy Country. He returned with armies granted to him by Wicked foreign powers, who feared the holy might of Antir and sought to ruin it with war.

Even in its diminished state, the holy armies of Antir still could not be matched. The Queen and her heir rode with their armies against the wicked usurper. Merciful Áine was a healer and a guide, but their mother the Queen was a fierce warrior unmatched upon the fields of battle. So it was that in the terrible battle that ensued, the Queen met the traitor in noble combat. By the justice of her divine rights, she slayed her wicked nephew.

Áine saw this and despaired. They pled to the heavens, "Oh great goddess, why must such senseless violence be in this world?" And Lucent Lumina, lady of all things pure and just, heard their plea and answered.

The heir accepted, and the Truth of all things was made known to them. Such divine revelation transmuted their body, mind, and soul, such that they might be a more perfect vessel for it. Thus the mortal heir of Antir was no more, reborn under the golden Sanctification of Lucent Lumina.

Thus transformed, pale skin laced with veins of pure gold, the heir illuminated the battlefield. To say that they were radiant would be untrue, for such words could not describe their brilliance. The nascent Saint shone like a second sun upon the battlefield, and all who saw them fell to their knees and wept. By the Truth that shone out from the Saint, the soldiers saw the great futility of their war. Each mortal upon the field saw the light of Lumina's Truth, and through it they could hear the thoughts of the nascent Saint.

*War is a waste of Lumina's gift of life upon the world*, they thought, and the armies listened. 

*But the ignorant shall never cease their wars willingly*, they continued, and the armies saw wisdom. 

*So we must show them the truth by whatever means necessary*, they decided, and the armies agreed.

The soldiers, all prior allegiances forgot, united as one under the Sanctified Monarch's banner of peace. Through their diligence and the awesome divine might of the Golden Saint, all nations before them saw the Light and the Truth, rejoining the Great and Holy Kingdom.
