*Book by Mage [[Zella Brightflower]], 949 BR*

... I will admit, when I am alone and fancy a pastime--one that allows me to temporarily forget my place in the Foxfire Tower--I take a walk to the Mirrorlake Clearing just outside the city wall attempt to measure the skies. Raeldan forbid a mother having a hobby or two when their child has grown enough feathers and leave the nest, so I kept this habit of mine away from the prying eyes of the stuffy academics within the Academy.

There will be those upon discovery of my pastime, would wonder why I didn't work with the Tower's Astrological Department. In truth, I could not bear the sin of asking the incessant and lousy twats in the Astrology College. Their science is not empirical, and most explanations are attributed to "the mysticism of the universe." The mages there actually believe that a too-careful observation of the heavens would bring Rhylea's wrath upon the surface, for attempting to scry too deep into the unknown is to anger the Mistress of Mystery. If it was not for the endowment they bring by "reading the heavens" for witless nobles, I would have had them extricated from the Tower ages ago.

It is of my opinion then that observation should be an art, one enacted with intent and precision. If one is going to observe something, one must do it systematically and religiously record their findings. To properly measure the heavens, I have toiled over redesigns of my existing arcane tools, then ordering their make from the artisans at Alturon. I had to blend the tools within my existing shipment to hide my true intent, as I cannot imagine the stir it would bring if the Astrology quacks discovered what I was up to.

Unfortunately, one of my first findings about the heavens happen to agree with said quacks. The sky changes, but because *we* are on a rotating body. If one observes from a static location, one is bound to record an exact stellar configuration as one did some time in the past. This also means that astrology books on the topic are still regrettably useful in my endeavors. I must think of how to excuse checking out some books on the topic without rousing suspicion...

**Addendum, added 987 BR**

Something is wrong. Theoretically, one can observe an exact stellar configuration if one record at the exact same geographical location over some fixed period of time. However, this does not take into account the Moon, which occasionally obscure parts of constellations in its orbit. I have taken this into account, so I have recorded the Moon's trajectory, as well as the stars' luminosity levels to study the effects when their lights commingle. While looking back at the recorded data, I noticed an... antithesis to this. There is no easy way to put this: I believe that there is empirical data that suggest the existence of an... anti-Moon. Periodically, and with a fixed trajectory, stars would be *dimmer* within a patch of the sky with a vaguely circular manner. I must study this further.

**Addendum, added 999 BR**

Nothing works. The Veil of Heavens refused to give in to me, and so I am stupefied. Nothing can be observed about this celestial "entity" except for its slight dimming effect on other entities. I often wonder if there is even such an entity in the first place, or is this a divine punishment from lady Rhylea herself. I should stop this research.

**Addendum, added 1000 BR**

I could no longer observe this entity, even through luminosity readings. I... I must be going mad. Lady Rhylea, I beseech thee, please forgive me.
