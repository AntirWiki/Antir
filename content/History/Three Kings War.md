After the end of the three kings war, the famed poet Alais Hermaris depicted the three sons of High King Tarlach as three beasts in a great forest, fighting over the body of a mighty stag with a crown of antlers.
**[[Lasairian of Antir|Lasairian]], the Drake.** A man of ruthless brutality and monstrous pride.
**[[Faolan of Antir|Faolan]], the Wolf**. A warrior of stout honor and fierce loyalty.
And **[[Cianan of Antir|Cianan]], the Fox** A boy of quiet cunning and lethal pragmatism.

Three years into the war, Lasairian made the fatal decision to ally himself with the dragons who once called Antir home. This massive gain in power - and betrayal of the kingdom's legacy - led Faolan and Cianan to ally themselves to see him defeated. Four years later, the final battle between the three armies left Lasairian dead and Faolan fatally wounded. Cianan, the youngest, found himself the only survivor of a seven year conflict, and the inheritor of a nation broken in both body and spirit. 

The three kings war began in 946 BR and ended in 953 BR.
