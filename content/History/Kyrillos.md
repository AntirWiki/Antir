Kyrillos is a drakos [[Lich]] who was sealed beneath [[Antir]] for millennia before his awakening in 953 BR. 

He was a servant of the [[Autumn King]] from that point until the [[Black Autumn]], at which time his soul was imprisoned within a tree at the center of what would become the [[Silent Woods]].

# Description
During his encounter with [[Tooth and Claw]] in 953 BR, Kyrillos appeared as a drakos skeleton wearing long black and green robes with glowing green eyes.

# History
## The Drakos Empire
Murals on his tomb depict Kyrillos and his undead legions fighting against armies of Drakos, including their [[Dragon]] war-saints. He holds a great distain for the dragons, and his atheistic attitudes alongside his ambitions towards rule led to conflict with the Empire. When they were unable to fully kill him, the servants of the dragon-god Lucimater, goddess of light and flame, imprisoned him in his tomb in what would later become [[Luscgard]].

## Awakening
In 953 BR his tomb was discovered and unsealed by [[Yvonne Silvershield]], who sought to revive her liege [[Faolan of Antir]] using the magics in Kyrillos' spellbook. This roused the lich from his slumber, and he attacked Yvonne when she attempted to resurrect the dragon Ozymandias in an act of desperation.

[[Tooth and Claw]], agents of the king, found Yvonne and defeated her, then destroyed Kyrillos while he was still regaining his strength. They took his spellbook reliquary to deliver it to [[Cianan of Antir]], so he could study the magic within and destroy the lich's soul.

[[Ninyva O'Niamh]], refusing to allow Cianan the power of lichdom, made a deal with the Lord of Gilded Leaves (the archfae who would become the Autumn King). The fae took Kyrillos' soul captive, and re-wrote the book into a tome of plant-inspired soul magic.

## The Autumn King
*From recovered manuscript of “The Hærfest Affairs”*:

Kyrillos, Master of Whispers, was an expert in seizing opportunities. If no way forward was presented to him, he’d gladly open doors for himself by reaching through others to turn the handle. One does not become a Lich without the perfect storm of combination of a wisened scholar’s immense drive to push onward, and a failed, ignorant youth’s complete disregard for the consequences of your actions on other people. Unfortunately, it was that same coincidental gale of traits that led him into the ambitious hands of Ninyva O’Neave, and later the impulsive, short-sighted council of the Lord of Gilded Leaves.

Now this was a door that was _begging_ to be opened. Ripe with delicious fear and crippling insecurity. The Lich had no physical form after being drawn out of the book, but was trapped in the Lord’s vicinity all the same. A relentless itching at the back of the mind. A consequence the Lord had taken willingly, both to protect his new pylon into mortal dealings, and to attempt to satiate that gnawing curiosity that befall all fey that witness the cycle of the seasons enough to start to find boredom in it. The Lord couldn’t help but eventually break the seal and start conversation between them. All Kyrillos had to do was offer a single support pillar to this crumbling tower, and the Lord of Gilded Leaves, a powerful archfey and ancient spirit, fell into the trap of an almost-mortal hubris, and tumbled straight into the Lich’s waiting hands.

Years of subtle dealings under the guise of tutelage. A hand of extended collaboration that tied puppet strings around trusting fingers. Promises of repairing his reputation in the Court after the victory of Cianan, and even providing a way to cull mortals who had grown too wise and strong against the chaos of the seasons and had begun to disregard their power. Delusions of grandeur were so much easier to produce in those that had already had a taste of both power and the loss of it. A tenuous waiting game played against the clock of his essence slowly dissipating into the feywild without a true phylactery or consenting anchor, but oh how he played it. This went beyond the capabilities of Lich-hood. If he played his cards right, he could become a new god. Kyrillos vowed to visit the grave of Ninyva some day to feed upon her soul in a twisted gratitude for the opportunity she had given him.

Maintaining facades. A benevolent lord, generous with gifting his blessings upon followers. The occasional Druid under his banner coming to the Major Court to complain… hearing the relentless vengeful whispers of plants and animals that had unfortunately passed within their training as healers of the land. They were dismissed and told to make peace with their failures. Eventually, the final knot was tied, and the question could be asked without fear of even subconscious hesitation. _“Make me a body.”_ And so it was done.

The sky crackled green in bursts across Antir that night as Fey combined with Lich to create something both unholy and entirely unknown. The king had learned from what had gone wrong with Ninyva’s grand design, and had improved upon it. A great and ancient oak, the Lord’s prized possession within his domain, was to be given life & limb, and become the host. Kyrillos, however, had stretched his hands further into the Lord than his panic and pride had let him feel. So, when the last drops of consciousness from Kyrillos were gifted upon the tree, the Lich pulled upon his puppet strings and snatched the Lord in with him. _Thus, The Autumn King was born._ A monstrous uprooting as the tree both grew and died. Every step crushing entire neighborhoods, and leaving sharpened, decaying splinters of rotted and fetid wood to poison the earth it pierced into. Two minds unnaturally joined in a single form. The Lord’s agony mixed with the Lich’s unending Lust for Power. Much to Kyrillos’s terror, it was entirely impossible for either side to come out entirely on top. Instead, major fears and emotions guided the creature’s moments. It became a force of nondescriminate destruction, withering everything and everyone its wide and snaking roots could touch. The lord’s domain, and those within it, fell quickly and silently to an oppressive decay, A quiet, corrupted death for a great House, as if purposefully spurning the loud and prideful nature of its leader. Once the Lord’s domain was crumbled beyond recognition, the malaise spread….unidirectional, as if catching onto an idea battled within its twin-mind, and following it as order. It rotted away the barriers to the feywild gate near Catse Crossing, and gave the creature access to the mortal plane.

The Black Autumn fell upon Antir. Careening towards the city, the crown jewel of the empire…. Memories fought and tangled but the city shone like a pristine beacon to its befuddled mind. Alturon. The last place an altered grimoire & powerful fallen witch were both seen, the Autumn King burned with a lust to reclaim, and tainted everything in its wake to get there. It had to be in Alturon. As for Kyrillos… it is written to be impossible to truly destroy a Lich… especially one who almost tasted the power of a god… However, the mind is more easily persuaded than the form. The violent separation of the Lord from Lich was the final straw needed to rip back all the anchors and entangle them around Kyrillos’s own consciousness, reducing him to nonsentience. Scholars argue whether the unconscious overwhelming evil contained within the ever-smoldering remnants of that great oak in the tainted soil of the Silent Woods to the north of Alturon… is preferable to the mind of a former wizard tainted by necromancy that has a chance of being reasoned with. History has not given us time enough to tell, for now, that area is best to be avoided. While not a Lich nor a mobile force, A wrong and uncanny energy still emanates out from that tree. It seems that everything living that comes within its path unwinds into its base elements before returning to tell the tale. Birds spotted flying above it, wings falling off and plummeting into pieces of meat as they near its epicenter.

However, it is also said that evil seeks nothing more than to be known. A powerful host is still naught but a vessel for power in the end, and can be replaced just as such. Teaching this tale of caution is imperative, as ignorant scouts & treasure-seeking hopefuls have already lost familiars permanently to seeking within those woods for answers. Small glimpses before crying goes dark… Whispers of writing inscribing itself onto tree bark. Perhaps a lich’s book reborn, seeking to unload its knowledge into another mind… perhaps something else entirely.
